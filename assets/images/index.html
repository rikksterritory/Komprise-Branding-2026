Brand Essence

Komprise should feel clear, credible, and modern in every enterprise context

Komprise sits at the intersection of modern data intelligence and mature enterprise trust. The brand should never feel noisy, trendy, or overdesigned. It should feel clear under complexity, credible in high-stakes environments, and polished enough for executive-facing communication.

Brand Mood

Komprise is a modern enterprise brand that feels clear, credible, and composed in every interaction.

Whether the audience is executive, technical, or marketing-led, the system should communicate intelligence, maturity, and calm control.

Logo

Use the original Komprise lockups with disciplined contrast and spacing

The logo system should remain crisp, uncluttered, and unmistakably Komprise. Use the primary horizontal lockup by default, reserve the vertical version for narrow or formal compositions, and apply the mark only when space is limited or a compact identifier is needed.

Primary horizontal on light

Default choice for website headers, white cards, and standard layouts.

Reversed horizontal on dark

Use on saturated purple, deep gradient, or black-led brand moments.

Vertical lockup

Best for signage, cover panels, title slides, and narrow hero formats.

Mark only

Use when the full wordmark is already nearby or the format is extremely compact.

Maintain at least 1x mark-width of clearspace on all sides.

Do

  • Match the logo version to the contrast of the surface.
  • Preserve the original artwork, proportions, and colors.
  • Allow generous quiet space around every lockup.

Don’t

  • Stretch, crop, rotate, outline, or recolor the logo.
  • Place the color logo on busy or low-contrast imagery.
  • Use the mark alone as a replacement for the full wordmark in headers.

Let Komprise Purple lead while green, blue, orange, grey, and gradients support the story

Website UI System

Build digital experiences that feel modern, premium, and quietly powerful

The interface system should feel consistent with Komprise marketing: strong purple calls to action, restrained green emphasis, soft depth, calm surfaces, and confident enterprise layout patterns.

Buttons

Use rounded, touch-friendly buttons with clear states and disciplined emphasis.

Cards

Cards use white and cloud surfaces, 16px radius, and a subtle border-shadow balance.

Imagery and Graphics

Support the story with purposeful visuals, not visual noise

Imagery should make complex data themes feel modern and approachable. Favor real environments, elegant product framing, structured data visualization, and restrained gradient moments that amplify meaning.

Photography

Use clean, premium, enterprise environments with visible light, depth, and credible context.

Data visualizations

Use charts and diagrams that look analytical, not decorative. Keep colors purposeful and legible.

Iconography

Favor simple line-based icons with consistent stroke weight and calm spacing. The open-source Tabler Icons library can be used for iconography across website and product surfaces.

Gradient usage

Keep gradients subtle and strategic. Reserve the strongest blend treatments for hero or campaign moments.

Usage Rules

A concise operating system for keeping the brand consistent

These rules are meant to speed decisions up, not slow them down. When in doubt, prioritize clarity, contrast, restraint, and the original logo artwork.

Logo

  • Choose the correct light or reversed asset for the background.
  • Use the horizontal lockup first whenever space allows.
  • Keep breathing room around the mark and wordmark.

Color

  • Do not turn every section into a gradient-heavy statement.
  • Do not let green compete with purple for primary emphasis.
  • Do not use low-contrast grey on grey for body content.

Typography

  • Use strong editorial hierarchy with disciplined spacing.
  • Keep body copy readable, practical, and free of filler.
  • Use weight changes intentionally, not excessively.
